Household and Energy Setup 
Amber Fu gathered details about Ben Chase's household, noting it consists of two adults and two children in a four-bedroom house of about 2,500 square feet. Ben Chase confirmed having a full energy setup, including solar, a Powerwall, and EV charging. The family's schedule is constant Monday through Thursday, with occupants leaving by 8:00 a.m. and the children and Ben Chase's wife returning by around 3:00 p.m.; Friday is their day off, Saturday they are home a lot, and Sunday they are generally out until later in the day.

Motivation for Solar and Powerwall Adoption 
Ben Chase explained that they acquired solar for environmental and cost reasons, noting their family runs a solar company in the UK. They estimated a six-year payback period, but the system has exceeded expectations, generating more electricity than anticipated, resulting in a current electricity bill of negative $80, even with EV charging. The EV charging was acquired first, about six or seven years ago, while the solar and Powerwall were installed together only one year ago. Ben Chase attributed the need for a Powerwall to the change in feed-in tariffs under NEM3, which significantly reduced the buy-back rates for exported solar electricity, making storage essential to avoid buying expensive on-peak electricity in the evenings.

System Management Challenges and Desired Features 
Ben Chase identified a major challenge with the current energy setup as the lack of effective management software, especially the incompatibility between Tesla and other EV systems, requiring constant manual adjustments. They use two EV chargers, a universal Tesla wall connector and a Juice Box level two charger, to manage charging three electric vehicles by constantly juggling which car charges when, which they find bothersome. Ben Chase stated that having an automatic system that could prioritize which EV charges first, when, and how much would be "fantastic" and would ideally require "AI to figure out" the complicated logistics with solar, vehicles, and the house.

Electricity Usage Patterns and Highest Consumption 
The household's resting electricity usage is about 0.3 kilowatts at night, with a pool pump adding 1 kilowatt in the middle of the day. The largest consumption is the HVAC system, which can draw up to 4 kilowatts when it kicks in during the summer afternoons. Ben Chase noted the highest electricity use occurs in the summer from approximately 4:00 p.m. to 9:30 p.m., aligning with their utility's peak hours (4:00 p.m. to 9:00 p.m.). Ben Chase confirmed they are on a time-of-use tariff with Ed SC and, before solar, they adjusted activities like charging and laundry to off-peak hours, but now their focus is on maximizing the use of self-generated solar energy.

App Usage and Future System Integration 
Ben Chase primarily uses the Tesla app for system management but also uses separate apps for their Juice Box EV charger and the Enphase inverters for the solar system. Ben Chase expressed a strong willingness to pay for an application that could consolidate control and data from all their devices—Edison, Tesla, home devices—into one place for comprehensive management. They conveyed excitement about the prospect of a smart system that automatically handles EV charging, AC, and solar usage, stating they would "love it," as the current situation involves too many different, non-communicating systems. Ben Chase offered Amber Fu the opportunity to view their system and data anytime to aid in their research.
